HANet: Hierarchical Augmentation for Continual Few-shot Event Detection

1. Problem Definition & Motivation

In modern NLP, learning new event types sequentially without forgetting previously learned ones (Continual Learning) is a major challenge. This problem is further exacerbated in Few-shot scenarios where only a handful of examples are available for new classes. I chose to implement HANet because it addresses this duality: balancing the preservation of old knowledge with the efficient acquisition of new, sparse information. My interest lies in how we can make AI systems more "life-long" learners without the need for massive computational retraining.

2. Methodology & Implementation

My implementation focused on the Hierarchical Augmentation strategy. I utilized:

  • Data-level Augmentation: Using back-translation and paraphrasing to expand the small support set.
  • Feature-level Augmentation: Applying noise injection and manifold mixup in the latent space.
  • Framework: Developed using PyTorch Lightning for modularity and experiment reproducibility.

3. Experiments & Results

Testing was conducted on the MAVEN dataset. I focused on:

  • Evaluating Forgetting Rate as a key metric alongside F1-score.
  • Benchmarking against a Simple Fine-tuning baseline to quantify the performance gains of hierarchical augmentation.
  • Analyzing the impact of different memory buffer sizes for rehearsal.

4. Critical Reflection & Next Steps

While the model showed significant improvement over baselines, I observed that feature augmentation can sometimes lead to drift in the representation of base classes. If I were to continue this research, I would explore Orthogonal Subspaces to further isolate task-specific features. This experience has deepened my understanding of the trade-offs in stability-plasticity and reinforced my desire to study advanced regularization techniques in graduate school.
