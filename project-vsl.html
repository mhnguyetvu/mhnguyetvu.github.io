Vietnam Sign Language (VSL) Recognition

The Challenge

Recognizing Vietnamese Sign Language (VSL) is a crucial step towards building inclusive technology for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community in Vietnam. Unlike mainstream languages, VSL has unique spatial and temporal patterns that require specialized Computer Vision techniques.

This project explores real-time gesture recognition to translate signs into text, easing communication barriers.

Technical Approach

  • Utilized MediaPipe for hand landmark detection to reduce background noise.
  • Implemented 3D CNNs and LSTMs to capture the temporal movement of signs.
  • Curated a specialized dataset of VSL gestures for training.

Future Work

Planning to expand the vocabulary and integrate the model into a mobile application for real-world usage.
